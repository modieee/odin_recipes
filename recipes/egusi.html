Egusi Soup

A delicacy from west Africa Nigeria, made from melon seeds. It is eaten by almost all tribes of the country and usually paired with other food stables such as eba, fufu, rice, etc.

Ingredients

  • Egusi(Melon Seeds)
  • Palm oil
  • Ugwu Vegetable
  • Meat(beef/chicken. anything of your choosing)
  • Pepper(fresh or powder, either is okay but preferable fresh)
  • Onions
  • Seasoning(maggi or royco)
  1. Prepare your Ingredients, blend fresh pepper and onions together and use a portion of it to boil your meat, add Seasoning to the boil to give the meat and the meat stock flavorful taste. wash your Ugwu vegetables and ensure there are no dirty between them.
  2. pour your palm oil in a pot that has already been placed on fire for more than two minutes and it's already hot.
  3. when the oil is hot as well pour in your grounded melon seeds and stir to keep it from burning as it fries.
  4. Once it fries for more than 3 minutes pour in your meat stock that you had prepared earlier and add a little water to it if the meat stock water doesn't measure up to the fried melon seeds.
  5. let it boil for 5 minutes and then add in your assorted meat and other condiments you have prepared.
  6. let it boil for another 2 minutes and add in your washed ugwu vegetable leaves.
  7. after it boils for a while your Egusi soup is ready to be served.
